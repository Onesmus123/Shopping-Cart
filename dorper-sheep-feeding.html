Dorper Sheep Feeding

Proper feeding is essential for keeping Dorper sheep healthy, productive, and resilient. Here are three key guidelines every farmer should know:

1. Provide High-Quality Forage

Dorper sheep thrive on nutrient-rich forage such as grasses, legumes, and hay. Give them access to clean, fresh pasture free from weeds and contaminants. During periods of low pasture quality - such as winter or drought - supplement with hay or silage to meet their nutritional needs.

üí° Pro Tip: Rotate your grazing paddocks every 2-3 weeks to keep pasture growth healthy and reduce parasite risks.

2. Supplement with Concentrates When Needed

Although Dorpers can maintain themselves on pasture alone, additional feed may be necessary during high-demand periods like pregnancy, lactation, or rapid growth. Balanced concentrates - such as grains or formulated pellets - can supply the extra energy, protein, and minerals required for optimal body condition and healthy lambs.

3. Ensure Constant Access to Water and Minerals

Always provide a steady supply of fresh, clean water, as dehydration can quickly harm sheep, especially in hot weather. Offer free-choice mineral supplements containing essential nutrients like salt, calcium, and phosphorus. Mineral licks or blocks make this easy and help prevent deficiencies affecting health and reproduction.

üö∞ Note: Sheep may drink more in hot weather, so check water troughs twice a day during summer.

Bottom line: With high-quality forage, targeted supplementation, and reliable water and mineral access, Dorper sheep can remain healthy, productive, and resilient year-round.
